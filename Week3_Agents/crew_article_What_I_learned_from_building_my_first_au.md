# What I learned from building my first automation workflow

## Final Article

I recently built my first automation workflow, and I can attest that it's a daunting task, but also a valuable learning experience. The process of automating repetitive tasks has improved my job and productivity, and I'm not alone - 90% of knowledge workers have seen an improvement in their jobs due to automation.

The experience was a steep learning curve, but it was also an eye-opener in terms of the potential for automation to transform business processes. I was surprised by how much time and effort was being wasted on manual tasks, and how automation could not only improve efficiency but also reduce errors. A notable example is the implementation of FlowForma by LSTM, which provided a digital system for students and lecturers to submit travel requests. This streamlined communications and provided an audit trail for easy access to information, demonstrating how automation can improve efficiency and reduce manual errors in business processes.
